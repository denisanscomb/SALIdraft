SALI Governance (Simplified)

Last updated: 2025-12-01

SALI (Standards Advancement for the Legal Industry) is a non-profit, community-driven organization that creates and maintains the Legal Matter Standard Specification (LMSS). Our governance is designed to be lightweight, transparent, and predictable.

How SALI is organized

SALI’s work is coordinated through three layers:

  • Board of Directors (strategy + oversight)
  • Operating Committee (day-to-day program leadership)
  • Working Groups (draft and propose changes)

Most standards work happens in public repositories (e.g., GitHub) wherever practical.

Board of Directors Strategy & Oversight

What the Board does

  • Sets overall strategy and annual priorities
  • Protects the SALI brand and intellectual property
  • Approves licensing/IP policies and the Code of Conduct
  • Ratifies the annual LMSS stable release (each January)
  • Final escalation point for high-impact disputes and governance changes

Composition and cadence

  • President (Chair): Toby Brown
  • Ex officio (voting) directors: current Chairs of LMA, ALA, ILTA
  • Meets at least twice per year, plus as needed
  • Quorum: >50% of directors; decisions by simple majority

Operating Committee Day-to-day Operations

Current members: Jim Hannigan, Toby Brown, Kelly Harbour, Ron Friedmann.

What the Operating Committee does

  • Owns the LMSS roadmap, priorities, and release plan
  • Creates, coordinates, and closes Working Groups
  • Oversees repository maintainers (review/merge, tagging, release notes)
  • Resolves disputes within or between Working Groups

How decisions are made

  • Meets at least monthly
  • Quorum: 3 of 4 members (or >50% if membership changes)
  • Decisions by simple majority
  • Major decisions recorded in lightweight notes and/or DECISIONS.md

Working Groups (WGs) Focused Delivery Teams

Working Groups do the hands-on work: drafting new terms, guidance, crosswalks, tooling proposals, and other improvements for LMSS.

  • Open to contributors (subject to the Code of Conduct)
  • Each WG has a coordinator and an Operating Committee liaison
  • WGs propose changes, but do not change licensing/policy and do not approve breaking changes on their own
  • Each WG has a written charter (scope, outputs, duration)

How WGs decide (tacit consensus)

  • For minor, non-controversial items: a proposal is approved if no substantive objections are raised within a comment window (typically 7 days).
  • Unresolved disagreements are escalated to the Operating Committee.

LMSS release process Predictable & Stable

Stable release (once per year)

  • Every January: one stable LMSS release is published
  • Proposed by the Operating Committee and ratified by the Board

Beta stream (throughout the year)

  • After January, SALI maintains a beta stream for the next annual version
  • Accepted changes are merged into beta and may be tagged periodically

Fixes to the current stable release

  • The January stable release is largely frozen for the year
  • Only critical bug fixes or clearly non-controversial corrections may be back-ported
  • Back-ports require Operating Committee approval and clear release notes

Approval paths (at a glance)

  • Minor, non-breaking content (additive terms, clarifications, typos):
    WG (tacit consensus) → Operating Committee review/merge → typically next beta
  • New domains / term sets (generally non-breaking):
    WG proposal → Operating Committee approval → next beta → next January stable
  • Breaking / structural changes (renames, removals, major restructures):
    WG RFC → Operating Committee decision → Board review/ratification when high-impact → next beta → next January stable
  • Governance / licensing / policy changes:
    Draft → optional public comment → Operating Committee review → Board approval

Transparency, conduct, and licensing

  • Work is tracked via public issues and pull requests where practical
  • Decisions that affect users are recorded in notes and/or DECISIONS.md
  • A Code of Conduct applies to meetings and online collaboration
  • Licensing/IP is Board-approved; repository LICENSE files are the source of truth

Escalation and appeals

Normal escalation path: Working Group → Operating Committee → Board of Directors.

How to participate

  • Join or propose a Working Group
  • Comment on GitHub issues and pull requests
  • Test beta releases and provide implementer feedback
  • Suggest new terms, mappings, or guidance with a clear use case
